How to build a gaming PC

Reasons you may want to buy a gaming PC could be that you are wanting to upgrade your current pc because it is too old, you may want to start playing games or doing more intense tasks such as video editing etc. This website is designed to help people interested in upgrading and building PC's for your need.

First we should talk about the CPU or the Central Processing Unit, this is a small chip in every computer which is what makes your computer run. The CPU is what is sending information from one part of your computer therefore the faster the chip, the faster you will be able to do activities. For games such as Valorant or CS:GO they will actually get far more frames per second as they are more CPU intensive while games like Shadow Of The Tomb Raider is much more GPU intensive.
Next we should talk about the the GPU you are using which is short for Graphics Processing Unit. The GPU is one of the most important parts of any gaming PC and what it does is graphics cards are what renders the images from your computer over to your monitor, the more powerful the graphics card, the higher your ingame graphics will be. For example if I were to play a game called Shadow Of The Tomb Raider on my computer and I had a GTX 970 on Maximum ingame settings then it would give us around 45fps at 1080P but if I swapped my Nvidia Gefore GTX 970 out for an Nvidia RTX 4090 then I would easily get over 150fps on the same game at 4K. One thing you need to remember though is that most people wouldnt be playing at 4K and for games as demanding as shadow of the tomb raider FPS doesnt really matter as it isnt a fast paced shooter game like Valorant and CS:GO.
One of the absolute most important components of any PC is the motherboard, a motherboard is a platform which every component plugs into to work together. Depending on how much you end up spending on the motherboard, it may come with features like Wifi and bluetooth or even Thunderbolt if you are lucky. The one thing that you must know about motherboards are that they only work with specific components such as only some CPU's or RAM may work or be compatible with some motherboards.
